Programming Philosophy

This block blends hypertrophy and athletic performance across three phases with logical deloads and testing:

  • Block 1 (Weeks 1–4): Base & Hypertrophy — Moderate % work, higher reps, and foundational plyos/sprints build tissue resilience, movement quality, and work capacity.
  • Week 5: Deload — Volume reduced 40–50%; include technique singles and mini-tests to gauge progress without fatigue.
  • Block 2 (Weeks 6–9): Intensification — Sets drop and % rise. Contrast methods (e.g., squat then jumps) build power; accessories tighten around heavy compounds.
  • Week 10: Deload + AMRAPs — Lighter % plus a single AMRAP @70% to estimate e1RM; simple athletic checks maintain speed while reducing load.
  • Block 3 (Weeks 11–14): Peak Power/Strength — Heavy doubles/singles with low contact plyos and quality sprints; emphasis on bar speed and specificity.
  • Week 15: Testing — Distributed across the week (SQ/BP/DL/OHP + jumps/sprints/COD); minimal accessories to keep the nervous system fresh.

Progression model: top-sets/back‑offs for seasoned athletes (density & intent), RIR‑based effort (mostly 1–2 RIR), %s anchored to your 1RM or 90% Training Max for stability, and strategic deloads to drive adaptation without overreaching.

Athletic integration: Power primers (jumps/throws/sprints) come first for neural freshness; COD/plyos volume scales with phase. The strength work is chosen to support speed (bilateral + unilateral patterns, hinge/squat balance, trunk stiffness).

Blocks: (1) Base • (2) Intensify • (3) Peak • (15) Test
